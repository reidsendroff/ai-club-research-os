# 09 — Competition Playbook

---

## Why Competitions Matter

Competitions do two things nothing else does: they create a hard deadline, and they put your work in front of people who don't know you.

Hard deadlines force teams to ship something that works, not something that almost works. External judges — people who have no reason to be generous — give feedback that internal showcases never will.

The goal is not to win every competition. The goal is to enter every competition we're eligible for with something we're proud of, and to get better each year at understanding what judges look for.

## How Competition Participation Works

### Eligibility Check (September)
VP/COO reviews the competition calendar with all team leads at the September kickoff. Each team identifies which competitions they will target based on their project and team composition.

**Rule:** Every team must target at least one competition per year. Participating in zero competitions is not an option for a team in good standing.

---

### Submission Preparation

**Four weeks before any competition deadline:**
- Team Lead confirms the submission requirements and formats to VP/COO
- VP/COO confirms the team is on track (they've been seeing this at scrums)
- Co-Presidents are briefed on what's being submitted

**Two weeks before:**
- Live deployment is up and stable
- Written submission components are drafted (project description, impact statement, technical explanation — whatever the competition requires)
- Co-Presidents or VP/COO does a readthrough of written materials. Basic editing, not a rewrite.

**One week before:**
- Full submission packet reviewed by at least one person outside the team (fresh eyes)
- Any final changes made
- Submit early if possible. Never submit on deadline day if it can be avoided. Systems fail.

**After submission:**
- Team announces the submission at the next club meeting
- It gets logged in the club's competition record
- VP Content posts about it on social

---

## Submission Quality Standards

**On written components:**
- Describe what you built in one clear paragraph before anything else. Judges read hundreds of submissions. Get to the point.
- Explain the AI component specifically. Not "we used AI" — "we used GPT-4o to generate personalized study plans from Canvas assignment data."
- Don't overstate impact. "We think this could help students" is fine. "This will revolutionize education" is not.
- Proofread. Typos in a competition submission are an unforced error.

**On demos and videos:**
- Show the product doing something real, not a slideshow about the product
- Narrate what's happening — don't make judges guess
- If there's a live URL, test it the morning of submission. Not the week before.

**On team representation:**
- Every person listed on the submission contributed meaningfully to the build. If someone joined the team but didn't build anything, they are not listed.
- Co-Presidents are never listed on competition submissions unless they were genuinely part of the build. Running the club is not a contribution to a project.

## After Results Come In

**If you win or place:**
- Co-Presidents send a message to the full club immediately
- VP Content posts about it — specifics, not generic congratulations
- The win goes into the club's record for future grant applications and admin presentations
- The team presents what they learned at the next meeting

**If you don't place:**
- Find out if there's feedback available from organizers. Request it if there is.
- Team presents their project at the next club meeting anyway. The work happened regardless of the result.
- Document what you'd do differently in the team's project notes.

**No shame in not placing.** A team that entered a national competition is doing more than a team that didn't enter anything. What matters is that they built something and put it in front of judges.

## Building a Competition Record

The club's competition history is one of its most valuable assets for:
- Grant applications (funders want to see a track record)
- Admin proposals (Mr. Novak and administration respond to concrete results)
- Recruiting (students want to join a club that does things)

Secretary maintains the competition record. Every entry is logged — not just wins. The record includes:
- Competition name
- Year
- Team name
- Project name and one-line description
- Result (placed / didn't place / pending)
- Any notable feedback received

This record is updated within one week of any result.
