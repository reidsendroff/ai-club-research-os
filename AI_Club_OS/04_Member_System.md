# 04 — Member System

---

## Member Levels

Not every member is the same. The system recognizes three levels:

**Active Member** — Attends 75%+ of meetings, is on a project team, participates in scrums. This is the baseline for being in good standing.

**Leadership Member** — Holds an officer position. Attends 90%+ of meetings. Owns a defined area of the club's operations.

**Alumni / Advisor** — Graduated members who want to stay connected. No formal responsibilities. Can be invited to guest-present or mentor current teams.

---

## New Member Onboarding

Every new member gets a structured first two weeks. No exceptions.

### Week 1 — Orientation

The Secretary assigns an onboarding officer (rotates through VP/COOs) for each new member cohort.

**What happens in Week 1:**
- New member is introduced at the next meeting by name and one sentence about what they're interested in
- They receive the Member Starter Pack (see below)
- They sit in on a full meeting without any presentation obligation
- Onboarding officer checks in with them after the meeting: "What did you want to see more of? What confused you?"

**Member Starter Pack (distributed digitally):**
- Link to this OS (or a simplified member-facing version)
- Club Discord/group chat invite
- GitHub organization invite (once they have a GitHub account)
- Competition calendar for the current semester
- Project team interest form (3 questions: what have you built before, what do you want to build, what tools do you already know)

---

### Week 2 — Team Placement

Based on interest form responses, the VP/COO places the new member on a project team. Criteria:

1. Skill fit — put them somewhere their existing skills are useful from day one
2. Interest fit — put them on something they actually care about
3. Team balance — don't stack all the strong builders on one team

New member joins their team's scrum at the next sprint meeting. They don't present — they observe and introduce themselves to the team.

---

## Participation Requirements

### Attendance
- **75% minimum** to remain in good standing
- **80% warning threshold** — Secretary sends a written notice when a member hits 80% or below
- **75% floor** — Members below this at any point are on probation: ineligible for competition representation, spotlight presentations, and leadership consideration

Excused absences: Secretary marks an absence as excused when a member notifies VP/COO at least 24 hours in advance and provides a reason. Two excused absences per semester maximum.

### Scrum Participation
- Every active member on a project team presents at every scrum
- Missing a scrum without 48-hour advance notice = 1 strike
- Presenting with nothing to show = allowed once per semester, logged, triggers a check-in

### Strike System

| Strike Count | Consequence |
|---|---|
| 1 | Logged. No action. |
| 2 | VP/COO sends a brief, direct check-in message. "Hey, noticed X — anything blocking you?" |
| 3 | 1:1 meeting with VP/COO. Required, not optional. |
| 4 | Membership review. President, VP/COO, and member meet. Options: resolve the issue, adjust expectations, or exit the club with no hard feelings. |

Strikes reset at the start of each semester.

---

## Offboarding

Members leave for all kinds of reasons. Most departures are fine and expected.

**Voluntary departure:** Member tells any officer they're stepping back. Secretary logs it. No drama. They're always welcome to return.

**Removal:** Happens after 4 strikes or a serious conduct violation. Co-Presidents make this call. It is always a conversation first, never a surprise announcement.

**Graduation:** Seniors are automatically transitioned to Alumni status. Their project documentation stays in the club GitHub. Their contact info goes into the Alumni roster that the incoming President maintains.

---

## Member Growth Path

The goal is for every engaged member to grow from observer to builder to leader within one to two years.

**Year 1 member (new):**
- Joins a project team
- Learns the tools (GitHub, APIs, whatever their team uses)
- Presents at scrums
- Attends competitions as a team member

**Year 2 member (established):**
- Leads a project team or leads a section of a larger project
- Presents at a leadership feature segment
- Mentors a Year 1 member on their team
- Is considered for officer nominations

**Year 3 member (leader):**
- Holds or has held an officer position
- Has competition submission history
- Knows how the club's infrastructure works
- Is actively preparing their successor

This path is not automatic. It requires showing up, building things, and taking responsibility. But it is available to any member who wants it.
