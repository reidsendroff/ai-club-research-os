# 03 — Meeting Runbook

---

## The Golden Rule of Meetings

**Members own the content. Leadership owns the structure.**

If Reid or Ben is filling the agenda every week because nobody else has anything to show, the club has a structural problem, not a content problem. The scrum system exists specifically to prevent this.

---

## Meeting Types

There are three types of meetings. Each has a different format.

1. **Standard Meeting** — Every week. Announcements, one leadership feature, member spotlight, work block.
2. **Scrum Sprint** — Every other week (alternating with standard). All teams present progress. No exceptions.
3. **Special Event** — Guest speaker, showcase, hackathon prep. Run on a custom agenda.

---

## VP/COO Pre-Meeting Checklist

Complete this 48 hours before every meeting:

- [ ] Confirm who is presenting and what they're covering
- [ ] Build the agenda and distribute to all officers via group chat
- [ ] Confirm Room 133 is booked and accessible
- [ ] Confirm AV/screen is working (do this day-of, not 48 hours before)
- [ ] Confirm VP Content has the announcement information they need
- [ ] For scrum meetings: confirm all teams know they're presenting and have the format

---

## Standard Meeting Format (60 minutes)

### 0:00–5:00 — Announcements (Secretary)
The Secretary runs this segment. Not the Presidents. Not the VP/COO.

What to cover:
- Competition deadlines in the next 30 days
- Club housekeeping (new members, changes to open lab schedule, anything administrative)
- Quick preview of what's happening today (30 seconds — not a full intro)

**Do not:** Turn this into a 10-minute recap of the previous meeting. Five minutes. Hard stop.

---

### 5:00–20:00 — Leadership Feature (Officer or Invited Presenter)
One officer or an invited technical presenter covers a tool, technique, or concept.

Format requirements:
- **Live demo required.** No slides-only presentations for technical topics.
- One concrete thing the audience can try before the next meeting.
- If you're explaining a concept, show code or output — not just diagrams.

Rotation: Cycle through officers so no single person is presenting every week. VP/COO maintains the rotation schedule.

---

### 20:00–45:00 — Member Spotlight (1–2 Teams or Individuals)
This is the main event. Members present what they built.

**Who presents:** VP/COO assigns this two weeks in advance. No surprises.

**What's expected from presenters:**
- What you built or improved (be specific — not "I worked on my project")
- One technical challenge you hit and how you got through it
- A live demo or screenshot — something visual, something real
- What you're building next (specific, not vague)

**What VP/COO does during this segment:**
- Keeps time. 10–12 minutes per team. Enforce this.
- Asks one follow-up question per presenter to show the room what good curiosity looks like.
- Notes any blockers the presenter mentioned that leadership can help with.

---

### 45:00–57:00 — Work Block (All Teams)
Teams work. Officers circulate.

**What officers do:**
- Move through the room. Check in with each team.
- Ask "what's blocking you?" not "how's it going?"
- If two teams are working on related things, introduce them.
- If a team is stuck, sit down and work through it with them — don't just tell them to Google it.

**What officers do NOT do:**
- Cluster at the front and talk to each other
- Leave the room
- Get on their phones

---

### 57:00–60:00 — Close (VP/COO)
VP/COO wraps up.

Script (literally say this):
> "Here's what we need from everyone before next meeting: [list 2–3 specific things]. Next meeting is [date]. [Scrum/Standard]. If you have blockers, DM me or Reid or Ben. See you [day]."

That's it. No long speech. No motivational close. Just what's next and where to find help.

## Scrum Sprint Format (60 minutes)

Scrum meetings replace the standard format every other week.

### Structure

**0:00–3:00 — Setup (VP/COO)**
Brief reminder of scrum format. Every team has 5 minutes. Clock starts when they start.

**3:00–53:00 — Team Presentations (All Teams)**
Each team presents in pre-assigned order. VP/COO tracks time.

**Required scrum format for every team:**
1. What we shipped or improved since the last meeting (not what we planned — what actually happened)
2. The hardest technical problem we hit and how we solved it (or that we haven't yet)
3. A live demo or screenshot
4. Our specific commitment for the next sprint (one sentence, measurable)
5. Blockers that need leadership help

**If a team is not ready:** They present anyway. "We didn't make progress" is an acceptable update. It goes on the record. Two consecutive no-progress updates triggers a 1:1 with the team lead and VP/COO.

**53:00–60:00 — Debrief (Co-President)**
One of the Co-Presidents closes. Call out the best demo. Surface any cross-team connections. Remind teams of upcoming competition deadlines.

## Special Event Format

Used for: Guest speakers, end-of-year showcase, hackathon kickoff, admin presentations.

Build a custom agenda for each special event. Minimum requirements:

- Distribute agenda to all officers 72 hours in advance (not 48 — special events need more prep)
- Send VP Content the event details 5 days in advance so promotion can be heavier than usual
- Confirm guest details (name spelling, what they want to be introduced as, any tech needs) 24 hours before
- Assign a specific officer to greet and host the guest — not a random person who happens to be near the door

## When Something Goes Wrong at a Meeting

**Presenter doesn't show up:**
VP/COO fills with a shorter version of the work block. Do not improvise a presentation. Do not put someone on the spot. Extend work time and use the extra time for 1:1 team check-ins.

**AV doesn't work:**
The presenting officer demos from their laptop passed around the room, or verbally walks through the project with screenshots on their phone. Never cancel a demo because of AV. Adapt.

**Room isn't available:**
VP/COO is responsible for having a backup plan (hallway, cafeteria, outside). This should never catch the club off guard. Check room availability day-of by 12PM.

**Very low attendance:**
Run the meeting at full quality regardless. Members who come deserve the full experience. Low attendance meetings often have some of the best conversations because the ratio of questions to audience is better.

---

## Post-Meeting Protocol (VP/COO + Secretary)

Within 24 hours of every meeting:

- [ ] Secretary updates attendance records
- [ ] VP/COO sends a brief "what happened + what's next" message to the full member group chat
- [ ] VP Content posts the You Missed It announcement
- [ ] VP/COO notes any team blockers that need follow-up and assigns them to an officer
- [ ] Any strike or attendance warnings triggered by this meeting are logged
