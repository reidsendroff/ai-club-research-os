# 06 — Announcement Playbook

---

## The System

Every meeting generates three posts. VP Content owns all three. They go out on a fixed schedule:

| Post | When It Goes Out | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 7AM Hook | Day of meeting, 7:00–7:30AM | Create anticipation. Get people talking before first period. |
| 1PM Reminder | Day of meeting, 12:45–1:15PM | Convert fence-sitters. Full detail. Highest-conversion post. |
| You Missed It | Within 3 hours of meeting ending | Social proof. Recruitment. FOMO engine. |

Miss any of these and you've left attendance on the table. All three, every time.

---

## The Non-Negotiable Rules

1. **No em dashes.** Use a comma, a period, or restructure the sentence.
2. **No "Thank you for your time."** Never.
3. **No formal language.** If it sounds like a school newsletter, rewrite it.
4. **7AM post has no time reference.** No "today at 2:45" in the morning. Room and time go at the bottom only.
5. **Specificity beats hype.** "Reid built a live image editor that responds to natural language prompts" beats "we're covering AI tools."
6. **Sign off with names.** Reid & Ben, or the full team when relevant.
7. **Room 133 @ 2:45** is always the format. Exactly that format.

---

## 7AM Hook Template

```
[One sentence on the most exciting thing happening today. Lead with what, not who.]

[One sentence on a second item if it's worth mentioning.]

[Optional: one line on anything special — guest, new format, competition result.]

Room 133 @ 2:45.

— Reid & Ben
```

**What makes a strong 7AM post:**
- The first sentence would make someone look up from their phone
- It's under 80 words
- It doesn't explain — it teases
- Room and time are the last thing, not the first

**Example:**
```
Reid built a live image editor inside MyClassPal that takes a homework prompt and generates a diagram. No Photoshop. Just the API and a few hundred lines of code. He's showing the full build today.

Ben is also walking through how he set up Claude Routines to auto-summarize class notes in under 10 lines.

Room 133 @ 2:45.

— Reid & Ben
```

---

## 1PM Reminder Template

```
Reminder — AI Club is today at 2:45 in Room 133.

Here's what we're covering:

[PRESENTER] — [DEMO NAME]: [2–3 sentences. What they built, what's interesting about it, what you'll walk away knowing.]

[PRESENTER] — [DEMO NAME]: [2–3 sentences.]

[PRESENTER] — [DEMO NAME]: [2–3 sentences.]

[Optional: one line about anything special.]

Room 133 @ 2:45. [One sentence closing that answers "why come today specifically."]

— Reid & Ben
```

**What makes a strong 1PM post:**
- Each demo description is concrete enough that someone could decide if they care
- It's detailed but not long — under 200 words total
- The closing line is honest, not hype
- It reads like a person wrote it, not a club account

---

## You Missed It Template

```
You missed a good one.

[1–2 sentences on the highlight of the meeting. What actually happened — not the agenda, the moment. What surprised people? What caused a reaction?]

[1–2 sentences on a second standout moment if there was one.]

[Optional: one-line paraphrase of something memorable that was said.]

Next meeting is [date / "in two weeks"]. We're covering [tease — one specific thing, not a category].

Follow along or show up. Room 133 @ 2:45.

— Reid & Ben
```

**What makes a strong You Missed It post:**
- It's written like a recap, not a repeat of the agenda
- Something specific happened — a demo that surprised people, a question that turned into a 10-minute conversation, a member who showed something nobody expected
- The tease for next meeting is specific enough to be a reason to come
- Tone is "here's what you missed" not "shame on you for missing it"

## VP Content Weekly Workflow

**Monday (week of meeting):**
- Get agenda from VP/COO (should already be distributed — follow up if not)
- Draft all three posts. Hold them. Do not post yet.
- Send drafts to Co-Presidents for a quick read. 24-hour turnaround max.

**Day of meeting, 7AM:**
- Post the Hook. Screenshot confirmation. Send to club group chat.

**Day of meeting, 12:45–1:15PM:**
- Post the Reminder. Same as above.

**Day of meeting, within 3 hours of meeting ending:**
- Write the You Missed It based on what actually happened (not the planned agenda)
- Post. Screenshot. Send to group chat.

---

## Special Situations

**Guest speaker:**
Promote 3–5 days in advance with a heavier post (not part of the three-post system — this is additional). Include who the guest is and why they're worth coming to see. The three-post system still runs on meeting day.

**Low-attendance meeting:**
Do not skip the You Missed It. A post that says "small group today, great conversations" is more compelling than silence.

**Meeting is cancelled:**
VP Content posts a 1–2 line update as soon as the cancellation is confirmed. Include when the next meeting is. Do not just go silent.

**Something amazing happened that wasn't planned:**
The You Missed It should lead with that. Plans are a guide. What actually happens is the story.

---

## How to Brief VP Content

VP Content needs the following to write great posts. VP/COO provides this 48 hours before each meeting:

- Who is presenting and what exactly they're covering (one sentence per presenter)
- What's new, surprising, or specifically interesting about this meeting vs. the last one
- Any guests, special announcements, or changes to format
- Anything a member said recently about their project that was quotable or interesting

The better the brief, the better the posts. Garbage in, garbage out.
