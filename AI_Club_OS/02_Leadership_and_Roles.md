# 02 — Leadership and Roles

---

## Leadership Philosophy

Leadership in this club is not a title. It is a set of responsibilities that someone owns every single week. If you are in a leadership position and the thing you own is not getting done, that is a leadership failure — not a member failure, not a scheduling problem.

Every officer has one primary job. Secondary jobs exist, but the primary job is non-negotiable.

---

## The Leadership Team

### Co-Presidents — Reid Sendroff & Ben Shamosh

**Primary job:** Set direction. Remove blockers. Represent the club to administration, parents, and the outside world.

**What they own:**
- Strategic decisions (what competitions to enter, how to structure the year, whether to change a major policy)
- Relationship with Mr. Novak and school administration
- Final sign-off on budget decisions over $50
- Approval of all external communications (emails to parents, outreach to sponsors)
- The club's identity — how it presents itself and what it stands for

**What they do NOT own:**
- Day-to-day meeting logistics (that's VP/COO)
- Attendance tracking (that's Secretary)
- Social media posts (that's VP Content)
- Budget line items under $50 (that's Treasurer)

**Decision authority:** Co-Presidents have final say on anything not delegated to another officer. In a disagreement between Co-Presidents, they discuss until they agree. If they cannot agree, the tie goes to the advisor.

---

### Vice President / Chief Operations Officer (2 officers)

**Primary job:** Make sure every meeting runs exactly as planned, every week, without the Presidents having to think about it.

**What they own:**
- The weekly meeting agenda (built and distributed 48 hours before each meeting)
- Room setup and AV/screen
- Member check-ins and the sign-in process
- Following up with teams who didn't present or missed a scrum commitment
- Scrum facilitation — keeping teams on time and on format
- The club's weekly rhythm — nothing slips because someone forgot

**What they do NOT own:**
- Content of presentations (that's the presenter)
- Attendance records (that's Secretary, though VP/COO helps enforce)
- Social media (that's VP Content)

**Accountability standard:** If a meeting runs poorly — wrong room, no agenda, teams didn't know what was expected — that is a VP/COO failure. Own it, fix it, don't let it happen twice.

---

### Vice President / Chief Marketing Officer (2 officers)

**Primary job:** Make the club visible. If someone at this school doesn't know what the AI Club is, that's a content failure.

**What they own:**
- All Instagram and TikTok posts
- The three-post announcement system (7AM, 1PM, You Missed It) for every meeting
- Recruitment content — posts designed to get new members into the door
- The club's visual brand — consistency across all posts
- Collaboration with Canva and any other design tools

**What they do NOT own:**
- Technical content of demos (they package it — they don't create it)
- Email communications to Mr. Novak (that's Presidents)
- Member accountability (that's VP/COO + Secretary)

**Accountability standard:** If there is a meeting and no announcement goes out, that is a VP Content failure. Three posts per meeting, every meeting, no exceptions.

---

### Treasurer (2 officers)

**Primary job:** Make sure money is tracked, distributed, and spent correctly. No team should ever not be able to build because of a funding problem that could have been solved.

**What they own:**
- The club fund ledger (updated within 48 hours of every transaction)
- API fund distribution to project teams at the start of Q2
- Monthly balance report shared with all officers
- Processing reimbursements for club-approved expenses
- Fundraising logistics (parent donation drive, any grant applications)

**What they do NOT own:**
- Budget approval over $50 (that's Presidents)
- Financial strategy (that's Presidents + Treasurer together)

**Accountability standard:** If a team can't access their API fund because of an administrative delay, that is a Treasurer problem. It should never take more than 5 school days to process a distribution.

---

### Secretary (2 officers)

**Primary job:** Track everything. The club's memory lives in the Secretary's records.

**What they own:**
- Attendance at every meeting (sign-in sheet, digital or physical)
- Attendance records updated in the shared ledger within 24 hours
- Written warnings to members falling below 80% attendance
- Competition deadline calendar, updated and distributed at the start of each semester
- Meeting minutes (brief — what was decided, not what was said)
- Strike tracking

**What they do NOT own:**
- Enforcement of strikes past the initial tracking (VP/COO handles conversations)
- Competition strategy (they track deadlines — Presidents decide which ones to prioritize)

**Accountability standard:** If a member disputes their attendance record and the Secretary doesn't have documentation, that is a Secretary failure. Every meeting must be logged.

## How Decisions Get Made

**Operational decisions (how a meeting runs, what format to use, scheduling):**
VP/COO decides. No need to consult Presidents unless it's a significant change.

**Content decisions (what to cover at a meeting, who presents):**
VP/COO in consultation with whoever is presenting. Presidents are informed, not asked.

**Financial decisions under $50:**
Treasurer approves. Logged in ledger.

**Financial decisions over $50:**
Treasurer recommends, Co-Presidents approve.

**Policy changes (participation rules, strike system, team structure):**
Co-Presidents propose, leadership team votes. Majority wins. Presidents break ties.

**Major strategic decisions (new competitions, partnership with outside organization, significant changes to meeting format):**
Co-Presidents decide, advisor is informed.

**Anything involving administration (new access requests, room changes, formal proposals):**
Co-Presidents own. No officer contacts administration independently on behalf of the club without President approval.

## Officer Elections

- Held in April for the following year
- All candidates must have been active members for at least one full semester
- Current officers nominate candidates; general members vote
- Results announced before the April showcase meeting
- Incoming officers shadow current officers for the final 4 weeks of the year
- Outgoing officers deliver a written transition document before their last meeting

---

## Leadership Failure Protocol

If an officer is consistently not meeting their responsibilities:

1. VP/COO (or Co-President) has a direct conversation. This is not a formal process. It is a conversation.
2. If the problem continues for two more meetings, a written note goes into the record.
3. If the problem continues after that, the officer is asked to step down or is reassigned to a role that fits their actual availability.

This is not punitive. People's lives change. The goal is to match responsibility to availability, not to embarrass anyone.
