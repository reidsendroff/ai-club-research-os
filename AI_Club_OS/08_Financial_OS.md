# 08 — Financial Operating System

---

## Philosophy

Money in this club serves one purpose: removing friction between a student and something they want to build.

No club fund goes to pizza, decorations, or leadership perks. Every dollar is traceable to a project, a competition fee, or a tool that members actively use.

---

## Annual Budget Framework

| Line Item | Target Amount | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| API Fund (5 teams × $60) | $300 | Distributed equally in October |
| Shared Tools & Licenses | $150 | Figma, hosting, domains shared across teams |
| Competition Entry Fees | $100 | Most are free; buffer for ones that aren't |
| Contingency Reserve | $100 | Never spent without Co-President approval |
| **Total Annual Target** | **$650** | Fundraise to this number |

---

## Fundraising

### Parent Donation Drive (Primary Source)
Runs in September. Target: $400–$500 from parent contributions.

**The pitch (use this language):**
> "Every dollar goes directly to a project team's API fund. Your student is building with the same tools used by engineers at Google and Anthropic. No overhead, no administrative costs, no pizza."

**How it runs:**
1. Co-Presidents draft the donation letter in August
2. Letter goes to all club member families via the school's communication system (or directly via email)
3. Treasurer sets up the collection method (school booster account, Venmo for the org, or equivalent)
4. Donation drive closes September 30
5. Total is announced at the October kickoff meeting

**Donor acknowledgment:**
Every donor gets a thank-you email from Co-Presidents within 5 school days of their donation. Not automated. Personal. Short. Specific about what their money will fund.

---

### Grant Opportunities (Secondary Source)
Several organizations offer grants specifically for student AI and CS clubs. Secretary maintains a list of these with deadlines. Co-Presidents are responsible for submitting at least one grant application per year.

**Known sources to check annually:**
- Google.org CS Education grants
- Anthropic education initiatives
- Local community foundations
- School booster organization's discretionary fund
- Congressional App Challenge — some districts provide stipends

---

### Sponsorship (Long-Term)
Not a Year 1 priority. In Year 2 or 3, if the club has demonstrated consistent output (competition results, live projects, membership growth), Co-Presidents can approach local tech companies or startups about a small sponsorship in exchange for recognition.

Rule: sponsors get their name on our posts and a thank-you at showcase. They do not get input on what we build, what tools we use, or how we run meetings.

## Budget Administration

### The Ledger

Treasurer maintains a shared Google Sheet accessible to all officers (read-only for non-Treasurers).

**Columns:**
- Date
- Description
- Category (API Fund / Tools / Competition / Contingency)
- Team or Officer (who it's for)
- Amount
- Approved By
- Running Balance

Updated within 48 hours of every transaction.

---

### API Fund Distribution

**Timeline:**
- September: Teams form, projects scope
- Mid-October: Treasurer distributes API fund to each team
- Monthly: Teams report usage to Treasurer (just a number — how much was spent)
- December: Mid-year reconciliation. Remaining budget reviewed.
- April: End-of-year reconciliation. Unused funds returned to general pool.

**Distribution method:**
Where possible, create separate API keys or sub-accounts per team. When that's not possible (some APIs don't support it), Treasurer manages a shared key and tracks usage via the API provider's usage dashboard.

---

### Expense Approval

| Amount | Approval Required |
|---|---|
| Under $25 | Treasurer approves |
| $25–$50 | Treasurer + one Co-President |
| Over $50 | Treasurer + both Co-Presidents |
| Contingency Fund | Both Co-Presidents only |

**No retroactive approvals.** If someone spends money without going through this process, the club is not obligated to reimburse it. This is not punitive — it's the only way to maintain budget integrity.

---

### Reimbursements

If a member or officer paid out of pocket for a club-approved expense:

1. Submit receipt to Treasurer within 10 school days
2. Treasurer verifies it was approved before the purchase
3. Reimbursement processed within 5 school days of receipt
4. Logged in ledger

No receipt, no reimbursement. No exceptions. This protects everyone.

---

## End-of-Year Financial Report

Treasurer presents a one-page financial report at the April showcase:
- Total funds raised
- Total funds spent by category
- Remaining balance
- Recommendation for next year's starting budget

This report goes to Mr. Novak and is stored in the club's shared drive for the incoming Treasurer.
