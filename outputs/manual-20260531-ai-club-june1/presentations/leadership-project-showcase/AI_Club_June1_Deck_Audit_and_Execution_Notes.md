# AI Club June 1 Deck Audit and Execution Notes

## What Was Wrong In The Current Deck

- The deck had the right design system and broad leadership structure, but the selection mechanism was too soft.
- "Vibe-coded AI project" sounded like one narrow artifact type. The actual bar is broader: create your best project with any tool.
- Presentation skill was underweighted. For leadership, this is a core signal, not a nice-to-have.
- The June 15 meeting needed to become the obvious proof-of-work checkpoint.
- The roles needed clearer fit logic: not every leader has to be technical in the same way, but every leader needs evidence of contribution.
- Reid and Ben's responsibilities needed to be clearer so officers understand they are not joining a title system. They are owning club functions.

## What Changed In The Final Deck

- Reframed the standard as: "Create your best project. Showcase it on June 15."
- Added the three leadership signals:
  - Grit
  - AI skill / build skill / coding or vibe-coding ability
  - Presentation and personable communication
- Added a June 15 tryout format:
  - What did you build?
  - Why did you build it?
  - What tools did you use?
  - What broke, and how did you fix it?
  - What would you improve next?
- Added a Canvas submission checklist.
- Added a revised leadership timeline:
  - June 1: process announced
  - June 8: guest speaker and build week
  - June 15: live project showcase
  - June 16: Reid and Ben review
  - June 17: decisions announced
- Added a selection scorecard.
- Added role-fit slides clarifying that different roles need different strengths.
- Added a Reid/Ben responsibility slide.

## June 15 Core Message

June 15 is the project showcase. Everyone who wants leadership should bring the best thing they have built and be ready to present it live. The meeting may run long, and that is fine. The point is to see evidence: who builds, who persists, who understands AI tools, and who can explain their work clearly in front of the room.

## Canvas Module Copy

Title:
June 15 Leadership Project Showcase

Instructions:
If you want to be considered for AI Club leadership next year, submit the best project you have built or can build before June 15.

This can be an AI app, website, automation, agent, game, data project, research prototype, or any other real project. You can use any tools: Claude Code, Codex, Cursor, Windsurf, Replit, ChatGPT, Gemini, Python, JavaScript, or anything else.

On June 15, be ready to present a short live demo.

Your demo should answer:
1. What did you build?
2. Why did you build it?
3. What tools did you use?
4. What broke, and how did you fix it?
5. What would you improve next?

We are looking for grit, AI/build skill, coding or vibe-coding ability, and presentation skill. Presentation matters because officers represent the club, teach members, and keep people excited about building.

Submission fields:
- Name
- Role or roles you are interested in
- Project title
- Project link
- Short project description
- Tools used
- What was hard or broken
- What you would improve next

## Role Criteria To Use In The Meeting

VP / COO:
Runs meeting ops, project-team check-ins, scrums, blockers, and follow-through. Needs reliability, organization, and people skills.

CMO / Social Media:
Creates announcements, posts, clips, project showcases, and speaker promotion. Needs communication, taste, consistency, and comfort with public-facing work.

Treasurer:
Tracks API budgets, parent donations, reimbursements, and project resources. Needs trust, organization, detail, and follow-through.

Secretary:
Owns attendance, notes, sign-ins, deadlines, Canvas records, and the AI Club Brain. Needs systems thinking, accuracy, and consistency.

TA / Admin Assistant:
Helps members during meetings, supports presentations, and jumps into messy tasks. Needs helpfulness, range, and energy.

Project Leads:
Lead specific builds, keep teams moving, and make demos real. Need technical ownership, grit, and communication.
