# GOALS_BACKCAST.md — Backward Planning from 2028

## Core Principle

Do not plan from today's task list. Plan backward from elite outcomes.

For every goal, ask: what would someone who achieved this outcome already have done? What evidence would prove readiness? What bottlenecks currently exist? What is the highest-leverage next step?

---

## The Outcome: June 2028

Reid Sendroff, age 18, receives a genuine offer from a Tier-1 frontier AI lab (Anthropic, OpenAI, DeepMind, xAI, or equivalent) upon high school graduation. He does not need a college degree to access this offer. He applies to MIT and elite universities as a parallel path — but the lab offer is the primary target.

**Estimated probability given full execution: 60–75%**

For this outcome to be real, the following must be true by June 2028:

1. Someone inside a frontier lab already knows Reid's work before any formal application
2. Reid has at least one objective signal of world-class capability (USAAIO gold or Camp; Codeforces International Master)
3. Reid has a public research artifact with a credible expert's name attached
4. Reid's GitHub and public profile are findable and immediately impressive to a technical evaluator
5. The lab offer path has been actively pursued — not assumed to materialize passively

## What Failure Looks Like — and How to Avoid It

**Failure mode 0: Too Many Good Things, Not Enough Great Things**
Reid already has broad strength across competitive programming, ML, products, and leadership. The biggest risk at this stage is not weakness — it is spreading attention across too many good signals and never producing one undeniable one. A profile with eight 7/10 accomplishments loses to a profile with two 10/10 accomplishments every time in a frontier lab evaluation.
*Mitigation: Actively cut. The four pillars exist to force this discipline. If something doesn't compound toward one of them, it doesn't matter how good it looks.*

**Failure mode 1: Diffusion**
Reid spreads attention across too many things — clubs, courses, competitions, projects — and goes deep on none of them. The profile looks broad but no single signal is strong enough to be undeniable.
*Mitigation: Apply the decision filters ruthlessly. Cut low-ROI commitments actively.*

**Failure mode 2: Optimizing for the wrong audience**
Reid's course selection, extracurriculars, and project choices are shaped by what looks good on a college application rather than what builds the four pillars.
*Mitigation: Every decision gets tested against "would a frontier lab evaluator find this impressive?" not "would an admissions reader find this impressive?"*

**Failure mode 3: Henstock relationship underdelivers**
Reid treats the Henstock projects as homework — delivers adequate work, misses the deeper opportunity to produce something publishable and to build a genuine professional relationship.
*Mitigation: Treat Henstock Project 1 as a professional engagement, not a school project. Ask good questions. Deliver more than expected. Show research taste, not just execution. Distinguish real research rigor from résumé theater — the difference is whether the output survives expert scrutiny.*

**Failure mode 4: Passive visibility**
Reid is impressive but not findable. No GitHub, no personal site, no public artifacts. A lab researcher who hears about him can't verify the signal.
*Mitigation: Public profile and GitHub are non-negotiable before summer 2026.*

**Failure mode 5: The window closes before Reid acts**
Reid waits for the perfect signal or the perfect preparation before pursuing the lab path actively. By the time he acts, the room has filled.
*Mitigation: The outreach phase begins in 2027, not 2028. USAAIO Camp connections and Henstock's network are the entry points.*

## The Parallel Path — MIT / Elite University

If the lab offer doesn't materialize by June 2028, Reid should be applying to MIT, CMU, and Caltech with a profile that makes him a compelling admit. The good news: the same preparation that earns a lab offer also makes him an exceptional applicant.

What admissions committees at these schools will see:
- USAAIO gold or Camp (rare, nationally recognized)
- Codeforces International Master (top 1–2% globally)
- Published or near-published clinical AI research with a Harvard Lecturer and industry expert
- Completed graduate-level ML coursework at Harvard Extension with distinction
- Founded two clubs; shipped real products; interned at an AI company

The fallback is not a consolation prize. It is a strong outcome that keeps the frontier lab path open for 2032 — though with a significantly higher bar given four years of additional AI capability growth.

## The Founder / Builder Option

A third path worth preserving: Reid has already demonstrated product instinct and execution ability (MyClassPal, ArgueMaster, Accelerado internship). If a compelling technical problem emerges between now and 2028 — one that justifies building a company rather than joining a lab — that option should remain open. The preparation for it is identical to the lab path. Don't close it off, but don't optimize for it at the expense of the four pillars either.
