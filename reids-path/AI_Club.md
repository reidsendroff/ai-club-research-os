## Announcement System

Three posts per meeting. VP Content owns all three.

### Post 1 — 7AM Hook

- Under 80 words
- No time reference in the body
- Lead with what's happening, not that something is happening
- Close: Room 133 @ 2:45. then — Reid & Ben

### Post 2 — 1PM Reminder

- Under 200 words
- Opens: "Reminder — AI Club is today at 2:45 in Room 133."
- Full detail on each demo: who, what they built, why it's interesting
- Close: Room 133 @ 2:45. then — Reid & Ben

### Post 3 — You Missed It

- Under 150 words, written within 3 hours of the meeting ending
- Past tense; lead with the best moment or what people were talking about after
- Tease next meeting with one specific thing
- Close: Room 133 @ 2:45. then — Reid & Ben

## Hard Rules (applies to all output)

- No em dashes
- No "Thank you for your time"
- No corporate language. If it reads like a press release, rewrite it.
- No motivational closers ("Let's build," "Stay curious" — all banned)
- No emojis as bullet labels; maximum one emoji if Instagram/Discord strongly calls for it
- No section headers or dividers in announcements or short-form content
- No "Hey Club Members"
- Specificity over hype. Facts do the work.

## Meeting Content

AI Club meetings run on a scrum format:

**Project demos / live builds:**
Members show what they built since the last meeting. Not slides. Working code, live demos, real
APIs. The benchmark: would this work outside of a school setting?

**Guest speakers:**
Promoted 3–5 days in advance with a dedicated post. The three-post system still runs on
meeting day.

**Open lab / workshop:**
Unstructured build time. Officers available to unblock. Used in the weeks between major demos.

## Tone Reference

**Wrong:**
"Join us for an exciting AI Club meeting where we will explore the latest developments in
artificial intelligence and work on our projects together!"

**Wrong:**
"hey everyone ai club today at 2:45 come check out what people are building"

**Right:**
"Reid finished the Canvas integration last night. It pulls live assignment data, generates a
personalized study plan, and updates automatically when the syllabus changes. He's doing a
full walkthrough today, including the part that broke twice before it worked.

Room 133 @ 2:45."

The difference: specific person, specific build, specific reason to show up.
