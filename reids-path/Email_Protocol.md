# Email Protocol (Admin / Mr. Novak)

The standard for every email sent to school administration or to Mr. Novak, the faculty
advisor. This applies to both clubs. The only difference is the sign-off.

## Rules

- State the ask or purpose in the first sentence.
- Under 150 words unless the request genuinely requires more detail.
- Bullet points for multiple asks.
- No em dashes.
- No "Thank you for your time."
- Professional but human. Not stiff, not a press release.
- Signed from the real people sending it.

## Worked example (AI Club)

> Subject: Two access requests for AI Club project teams
>
> Mr. Novak, we have two project teams ready to start building and need access to get them
> unblocked this week.
>
> - API budget approval for the two teams entering the Congressional App Challenge (details
>   in the attached one-pager)
> - A standing reservation for Room 133 on meeting days through the end of the semester
>
> Both teams already have working prototypes. The access is what stands between them and a
> real submission. Happy to walk through either request whenever works for you.
>
> Reid and Ben

## What makes this work

The ask is in the first line. The two requests are bulleted, not buried in a paragraph. It
closes with a concrete offer, not a thank-you. It reads like a person wrote it.
