## Meeting Content

CS Club meetings typically cover:

**Problem-solving sessions:**
Live walkthroughs of algorithmic problems. Officers explain their approach, not just the
solution. The goal is for members to understand the thinking pattern, not memorize the answer.

**Competition prep:**
Practice rounds timed to mirror competition conditions. Members solve problems independently,
then debrief as a group.

**Concept breakdowns:**
Topics like dynamic programming, graph traversal, binary search, greedy algorithms, sorting.
Always taught with a concrete problem as the anchor, never in the abstract.

**Language skills:**
Java fundamentals as they apply to competitive programming: Scanner for input, output
formatting, data structures. Members who want to go further with I/O optimization
(BufferedReader, etc.) are encouraged to explore that independently.

## Tone Reference

**Wrong:**
"Join us this Monday for an exciting CS Club meeting where we will be exploring competitive
programming strategies and sharpening our coding skills!"

**Wrong:**
"hey everyone meeting today @ 2:45 room 133 we're doing some cp problems"
(abbreviates competitive programming AND reads like a last-minute reminder with no substance)

**Right:**
"Today we're working through a graph traversal problem that tripped up most people at the last
USACO round. Matthew is walking through the approach that actually worked and why the obvious
solution doesn't. Room 133 @ 2:45."

The difference: specific problem, specific presenter, specific reason to care.
