# AI Club Announcement Templates

Copy, fill in the brackets, and post. Every template follows the three-post model.

---

## Template Set — Standard Meeting

### 7AM Post

```
[MEETING DAY] meeting is going to be a good one.

[PRESENTER NAME] is showing [SPECIFIC DEMO/PROJECT] — [one sentence on why it's interesting or what's surprising about it].

We're also covering [SECOND ITEM] and [THIRD ITEM IF APPLICABLE].

Room 133 @ 2:45. See you there.

— Reid & Ben
```

**Example (filled in):**
```
Today's meeting is going to be a good one.

Reid is showing how he built a live image editor inside MyClassPal using GPT-4o — no external tools, just a prompt and the model does the rest.

We're also covering Agentic Systems and how Claude Routines work in production.

Room 133 @ 2:45. See you there.

— Reid & Ben
```

---

### 1PM Post

```
Reminder — AI Club is today at 2:45 in Room 133.

Here's what we're covering:

[PRESENTER 1] — [DEMO NAME]: [2 sentence description of what they built or what they'll show and why it matters]

[PRESENTER 2] — [DEMO NAME]: [2 sentence description]

[PRESENTER 3] — [DEMO NAME]: [2 sentence description]

[Optional: one line about a guest or something special about this meeting]

Room 133 @ 2:45. If you've been thinking about coming, today's a good day to start.

— Reid & Ben
```

**Example (filled in):**
```
Reminder — AI Club is today at 2:45 in Room 133.

Here's what we're covering:

Reid — MyClassPal Live Demo: A full walkthrough of the Canvas integration, Mastery Mode, and the homework guardrail. This is a live demo of a product that's actually in production.

Ben — Claude Routines: How to build reusable AI workflows that run automatically. No code required to follow along.

Arjun — Social Media with AI: How the team is using Canva and Instagram to build the club's brand from scratch.

Room 133 @ 2:45. If you've been thinking about coming, today's a good day to start.

— Reid & Ben
```

### You Missed It Post

```
You missed a good one.

[1-2 sentences on the highlight of the meeting — what actually happened, not just what was planned. What surprised people? What was the best moment?]

[PRESENTER NAME] showed [SPECIFIC THING THEY SHOWED] and [one reaction or outcome — what the room said, what clicked, what question it sparked].

[Optional second item recap in 1-2 sentences]

Next meeting: [DATE OR "in two weeks"]. We're covering [TEASE OF NEXT TOPIC].

Follow along or show up. Room 133 @ 2:45.

— Reid & Ben
```

**Example (filled in):**
```
You missed a good one.

Reid walked through the full MyClassPal build live — from Canvas sync to Mastery Mode to the homework guardrail — and answered questions for about 20 minutes after. A lot of people didn't realize a student had shipped something this complete.

Ben broke down how Claude Routines work under the hood, and by the end of the meeting three people had already started building their own.

Next meeting is in two weeks. We're covering ChatGPT Images 2.0 and how to build image pipelines with the API.

Follow along or show up. Room 133 @ 2:45.

— Reid & Ben
```

## Template Set — Special Event / Guest Speaker

### 7AM Post

```
Something different today at AI Club.

[GUEST NAME] is coming in to talk about [TOPIC]. [One sentence on who they are and why that's relevant to what the club builds.]

[Any other agenda items in one line]

Room 133 @ 2:45.

— Reid & Ben
```

---

### 1PM Post

```
AI Club today — 2:45, Room 133.

[GUEST NAME] is joining us. [2-3 sentences on their background and what they'll cover. Make it concrete — what will people walk away knowing?]

[Presenter 2 and their topic in 2 sentences if applicable]

This one is worth being on time for.

— Reid & Ben
```

---

### You Missed It Post

```
[GUEST NAME] came to AI Club today and it did not disappoint.

[2-3 sentences on what they talked about and what landed. What was the most useful thing they said? What did it change about how people think about the topic?]

[Optional: one quote or paraphrase if something was memorable]

We'll have more guests like this throughout the year. Next meeting: [DATE/TEASE].

Room 133 @ 2:45.

— Reid & Ben
```

---

## Quick Checklist Before Posting

- [ ] No em dashes anywhere
- [ ] Room 133 @ 2:45 is in every post
- [ ] 7AM post has no time reference ("today at 2:45" removed)
- [ ] Demos are described specifically, not generically
- [ ] Sign-off includes names (Reid & Ben, or full team as appropriate)
- [ ] You Missed It is written past-tense with something real that happened — not a copy of the agenda
