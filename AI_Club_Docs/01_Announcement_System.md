# AI Club Announcement System

## Overview

Every meeting gets three announcements. Not one. Three. Each one serves a different purpose and hits a different audience at a different moment in their day. This is the system that has consistently driven attendance and engagement.

---

## The Three-Post Model

### Post 1 — The 7AM Hook
**Purpose:** Create anticipation before school starts. Get people talking about the meeting before they even sit down in first period.

**Rules:**
- Never mention the time. People check this in the morning — they know it's the morning. Saying "today at 2:45" is redundant and kills the energy.
- Lead with what's exciting, not logistics. The room and time go at the bottom.
- Short. This gets read in 10 seconds or it doesn't get read at all.
- Tone: hype but real. Sounds like a person, not a brand account.

**What to include:**
- What's being demoed or presented (be specific — "ChatGPT Images 2.0" not "AI tools")
- Who's presenting if there's a name worth dropping
- One line that makes it clear why someone would regret skipping
- Room and time at the bottom

---

### Post 2 — The 1PM Reminder
**Purpose:** Convert the people who saw the morning post but forgot. This is your highest-conversion touchpoint of the day.

**Rules:**
- More detail than the morning post. People who are on the fence want to know exactly what they're walking into.
- Still no jargon. Still human. Still no em dashes.
- Recap all three demos or agenda items with one line each.
- Room and time prominent — this is the post people screenshot to remember where to go.

**What to include:**
- Full agenda with short descriptions of each item
- Any guest speakers or special guests
- Room, time, and how to find it
- One closing line that reinforces why this meeting matters

---

### Post 3 — You Missed It
**Purpose:** Recap for people who weren't there. Social proof for people who were. Recruitment tool for people who haven't joined yet.

**Rules:**
- Written past-tense as a genuine recap, not a guilt trip.
- Highlight the best moment or most surprising thing that happened.
- End with a forward-looking hook — what's coming next.
- This post often gets more views than the pre-meeting posts. Write it like it matters.

**What to include:**
- What actually happened (not what was planned — what landed)
- Any standout demos, moments, or reactions
- Quote or paraphrase from a presenter if something was memorable
- What's coming at the next meeting
- Where to join / follow for updates

---

## The Non-Negotiable Rules

1. **No em dashes.** They read as formal and corporate. Use a comma, a period, or just end the sentence.
2. **No "Thank you for your time."** Never. It sounds like a LinkedIn post.
3. **Specific over vague.** "We built a live image editor using GPT-4o" is better than "we explored AI image tools."
4. **Room 133 @ 2:45** is always the format. Never just "the usual room."
5. **No time in the 7AM post.** It reads weird. Leave it out.
6. **Sign off with names.** Reid, Ben, and the team — not "The AI Club."

---

## Inputs Needed to Write a Set

Give me these and I'll write all three in one shot:

- What's being demoed or presented (be as specific as possible)
- Who is presenting each item
- Any guest speakers
- Anything new or surprising about this meeting vs. the last one
- Room and time (default: Room 133 @ 2:45)
- Any announcements or housekeeping items

---

## Tone Reference

**Too formal:**
> "We are pleased to announce that our upcoming meeting will feature a presentation on the latest advancements in generative AI technology."

**Too casual/vague:**
> "hey guys meeting today should be fun come if you can"

**Right:**
> "Reid's walking through how he built a live image editor that responds to natural language — no Photoshop, just a prompt. It's live on myclasspal.ai. Room 133 @ 2:45."
